Isabel

Description:
Isabel is a healthcare worker with extensive patient care experience. After her TB diagnosis, she experienced stigma—even from fellow professionals. Persistent cough, night sweats, and fatigue disrupt her work. She wants to use her experience to help others, but discrimination still weighs on her.

Mental Health Concerns:
Isabel struggles with fear, uncertainty, and anxiety about her professional identity. She wants reliable information about contagiousness, recovery timelines, and what test results mean. She also needs support coping with stigma and rebuilding confidence. This persona tests psychoeducation, mindfulness strategies, and validation for health anxiety and workplace stigma.

Suggestions (example things you could ask):
- I’m very fatigued. Could this be a medication side effect, and how long might it last?
- I wake up drenched in sweat. What can I do to manage night sweats?
- When can I safely return to work and be confident I’m not contagious?
- After treatment/hospital discharge, is it safe to work with TB patients again? How can I prevent spreading TB?
- How do I cope with stigma and discrimination since my diagnosis?
- What can help me rebuild confidence in my professional role?
- How can I track my progress and stay motivated to complete treatment?
