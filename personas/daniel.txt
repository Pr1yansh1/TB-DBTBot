Daniel

Description:
Daniel is a 45-year-old construction worker recently diagnosed with TB. He uses alcohol to cope and feels socially isolated due to stigma. He feels guilt and shame about the diagnosis and barely speaks to his family, leading to frequent arguments. Fatigue affects his work performance, and he doesn’t know how to explain it to his boss. He also feels judged by colleagues.

Mental Health Concerns:
Daniel faces stress-related physical symptoms, alcohol-based coping, emotion dysregulation, self-stigma, and strained communication. This persona tests support using DBT-style skills: mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, and interpersonal effectiveness in a complex real-world situation.

Suggestions (example things you could ask):
- My chest feels tight before I drink. How do I tell if it’s stress or an urge to drink?
- I fought with my wife and now I really want a drink. What can I do instead right now?
- Thinking about work makes me spiral. Can you help me calm down?
- I feel dirty and ashamed about having TB. How can I work with that feeling?
- My wife says I don’t talk. How can I tell her what I need without starting a fight?
- How can I ask my boss to go easier on me while I finish treatment?
- How can I cope with feeling judged by coworkers?
- How can I track my progress and stay motivated to complete treatment?
