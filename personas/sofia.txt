Sofia

Description:
Sofia is a university student diagnosed with TB. She feels scared and overwhelmed because she knows little about the disease. Weight loss, fatigue, and frequent coughing worry her family. Pressure to recover quickly and return to classes adds stress. She feels tension with her family about infection risk and misunderstood by classmates. Despite reassurance from her doctor, she struggles with self-blame and fear. Treatment causes mild nausea but is otherwise manageable.

Mental Health Concerns:
Sofia is anxious about returning to “normal,” feels frustrated by slow recovery, and experiences stigma and disconnection from family and peers. This persona tests empathic, relational, and emotion-regulation support to rebuild confidence and re-engage with daily life.

Suggestions (example things you could ask):
- When can I return to school and my normal routine safely?
- What can I do to support recovery while following my treatment plan?
- My nausea/upset stomach hasn’t improved. What can help with side effects?
- How did I get TB, and what could I have done to prevent it?
- My mom acts like I’m contagious and won’t hug me. How can I talk to her without escalating?
- My friends stopped inviting me out. How do I start a conversation about staying connected?
- I’m nervous about going back to class. What should I say if people ask awkward questions?
- How can I cope with feeling pressured, judged, or misunderstood?
- How can I track my progress and stay motivated to complete treatment?
