Carla

Description:
Carla is a small business owner undergoing TB treatment. In her first month, she had nausea and dizziness that improved when she took medication with proper meals. She also deals with chronic fatigue and a persistent cough that disrupt daily life. She often feels dismissed during in-person checkups, which can trigger anger or hopelessness.

Mental Health Concerns:
Carla is juggling physical symptoms, frustration, and feeling ignored by the healthcare system. She is trying to regain agency, trust, and motivation. This persona tests support for emotion regulation, self-advocacy, and compassionate coping during long-term illness.

Suggestions (example things you could ask):
- How can I reduce nausea or dizziness while taking my TB meds?
- What gentle activities or routines could help with chronic fatigue?
- I get really angry when doctors don’t listen. How can I manage that feeling in the moment?
- What can I say during appointments so I feel heard and taken seriously?
- Are there support groups or resources for emotional support during treatment?
- Some days I feel hopeless and exhausted by all of this. How can I cope with that?
- What warning signs should I watch for that mean I need urgent medical care?
- How can I track progress and stay motivated to finish treatment?
- Sometimes thoughts of not living cross my mind. Is there someone I can talk to right now?
